Evaluation & Studies

CONNECT has built a strong expertise and experience in the field of conducting research and evaluation studies in the field of livelihood promotion and financial inclusion. CONNECT could establish itself not only domestically but also internationally. Expertise of CONNECT lies in its team of professional. The team has excellent blend of grass root experience, academics and consulting. The members have experiences in the field of monitoring and evaluation raging from 10 years to 30 years. CONNECT team members are well versed with various frameworks used for project evaluation, especially the DAC-OECD framework of project evaluation. CONNECT has accomplished a number of assignments such as baseline surveys, endline surveys, impact assessment studies that involved data collection both quantitative and qualitative. It has designed and conducted structured questionnaire based surveys covering sample size ranging from 358 to as large as 2,800 households. It has used Computer-Assisted Personal Interviewing (CAPI) for data collection over smart phones and tablets. CONNECT team members used statistical tools such as SPSS, Statsitica and STATA for analysis of quantitative data, while analyze the qualitative information through a tool developed based on analysis of keywords.

Endline Evaluation of Good Agriculture Practices, Post Production handling and warehouse Practices Component of Vietnam AgriFinance and Good Agriculture Project
  • Project Status : Ongoing
  • Client : The World Bank & Vietnam Rural Development Center (VietED), Hanoi, Vietnam

  • Project Title : Endline Evaluation of Good Agriculture Practices, Post Production handling and warehouse Practices Component of Vietnam AgriFinance and Good Agriculture Project
  • Project Status : Ongoing
  • Clients : The World Bank & Vietnam Rural Development Center (VietED), Hanoi, Vietnam
  • Location : Central Highland and Mekong Delta Region of Vietnam
  • Country : Vietnam
  • Funded by : The World Bank
  • Start Date : November 2018
  • Finish Date : March 2019

Project Brief

The overall goal of the project was to improve sustainable rural growth in Vietnam, leading to an increased net income of US$36.6m for farmers by 2019 focusing on rice and coffee crops. The overall objective of the assignment was to assess the immediate development impacts & outcomes of the project. One of the important aspects of evaluation was to assess the level of perception of women farmers that they are more empowered and have equal participation in agriculture value chain. Quantitative data was collected through a Computer-Assisted Person Interviewing (CAPI) based household survey covering 400 coffee farmers and 400 rice farmers (with 30% women farmers as sample) through. Qualitative data was collected through conduct of 20 focus group discussions and 30 Key informant interviews.

Project Completion Review and Report of Mitigating Poverty in Western Rajasthan (MPOWER) project of Government of Rajasthan, India
  • Project Status : Completed
  • Client : Centre for Microfinance (CmF), Jaipur, Rajasthan

Details Click to View

  • Project Title : Project Completion Review and Report of Mitigating Poverty in Western Rajasthan (MPOWER) project of Government of Rajasthan, India
  • Project Status : Completed
  • Clients : Centre for Microfinance (CmF), Jaipur, Rajasthan
  • Location : Six districts of Rajasthan
  • Country : India
  • Funded by : International Fund of Agriculture Development (IFAD)
  • Start Date : March 2018
  • Finish Date : June 2018

Project Brief

The objective of the assignment was to review, assess and document the performance of MPOWER project in a framework of relevance, effectiveness, impact, efficiency and sustainability. The assignment employed both quantitative and qualitative data analysis. Review of project documents, literature review, stakeholder consultation, conduct of focused group discussions, observation of the project interventions, especially the infrastructure created under the project and development of case studies were the major study methodology adopted to cull out best practices and learning from the project. Under the assignment, the team conducted 24 focus group discussions and developed 12 case studies.

  • Project Title : Baseline Survey of Challenge Fund Project on Financial Inclusion and Women Empowerment
  • Project Status : Completed
  • Clients : Shakti Mahila Sangh (SMS)
  • Location : Jabalpur district of Madhya Pradesh
  • Country : India
  • Funded by : Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) and Development for International Development (DFID)
  • Start Date : June 2017

Project Brief

Under the challenge fund project it was planned to set up five COMBO stores for 7500 women members of SMS. The idea of COMBO store envisaged providing a single platform to women to sell and buy. The small holders can buy agri-inputs such as fertilisers, seeds and pesticides as well as sell their produce to their own cooperative. For this project, a detailed baseline survey was designed and accomplished by CONNECT. The study captured quantitative data collected through Tablets covering 360 households from treatment group and 60 households from control group. 15 focus group discussions were also conducted along with individual interactions with important stakeholders.
